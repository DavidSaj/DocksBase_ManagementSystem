6. Standard Clauses
  1. The berth holder agrees to comply with all marina regulations and bye-laws in force from time to time, copies of which are available from the marina office.
  2. The marina reserves the right to reallocate the berth in exceptional circumstances (including but not limited to safety requirements or major infrastructure works) with a minimum of 7 days' written notice to the berth holder, and shall endeavour to provide an equivalent alternative berth.
  3. The berth holder is responsible for maintaining adequate third-party liability and hull insurance cover at all times during the term of this agreement. Evidence of current insurance must be provided to the marina upon request.
  4. The marina accepts no liability for any loss of or damage to vessels, equipment, personal property, or any other item howsoever caused, whether by storm, flood, fire, theft, or any other cause.
  5. Either party may terminate this agreement by giving not less than 30 days' written notice to the other party. In the event of material breach by the berth holder, the marina may terminate immediately.
  6. This agreement is governed by the laws of the jurisdiction in which the marina operates, and the parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of that jurisdiction.
