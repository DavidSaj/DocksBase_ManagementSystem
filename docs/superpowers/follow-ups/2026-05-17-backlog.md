## Fast-follow (next sprint candidates)

### F1 — Stripe Connect payout-block for delinquent marinas
**Origin:** billing-gates spec reviewer suggestion (item 17 in open-questions).
**Why:** Today, delinquent-marina enforcement happens by blocking marina-admin from making new bookings (middleware). The boater portal stays open at every state (Trap 3 fix). The leverage gap: a marina can still RECEIVE money via Stripe Connect even while not paying us. The cleaner mechanism is to use Stripe Connect's payout-block on the connected account balance — funds keep flowing in (good for boaters) but stay frozen until the marina clears their software subscription debt with us.
**Scope:** add a service call that toggles `payouts_enabled=false` on the marina's `stripe_account_id` when `billing_state` enters `restricted`, and re-enables on restore. Add admin-override to bypass.
**Effort:** ~1 day.

**Origin:** billing-gates open question.
**Why:** Two parallel lifecycle fields with overlapping semantics. `status` is the old human-managed field; `billing_state` is the new automated field. Long-term, one field with clear sub-states is cleaner.
**Scope:** Migrate `status` values into `billing_state` namespace (or vice versa), update all readers. Test exhaustively.

### TD4 — Serializer-level `assert_marina_can` guards
**Origin:** billing-gates open question (item 20).
**Why:** Middleware is defence-in-depth but produces 402 with a generic error. Serializer-level guards give finer-grained UX errors ("This marina's subscription is suspended — bookings cannot be created").

**Effort:** ~0.5 day.

### TD5 — `Booking.member_id` does not exist (lead-score "never-booked" gate silently broken)
**Origin:** drive-by find during `Marina.max_loa` drop (column-drop agent, 2026-05-18).
**Why:** `recalculate_lead_scores` had a "never-booked" gate that referenced `Booking.member_id`. That field does not exist on the `Booking` model — the gate was raising `AttributeError`, being silently swallowed by an `ImportError`-only handler, and effectively never filtering anyone. Effect: every member got scored regardless of booking history, so the "never booked here" weight contributed zero signal to lead scoring.
**Scope:** Decide whether (a) `Booking` should grow an explicit `member` FK so lead-score can use it, or (b) the gate should be rewritten to derive the member via existing relations (e.g. `Booking.vessel.owner`), or (c) the gate should be deleted entirely if "never booked" is no longer a useful signal. Defensive patch is in place (degrades to "score everyone") so this is non-urgent — but the lead-score output is currently noisier than intended.
**Effort:** ~0.5 day depending on path chosen.

### TD6 — `Vessel.is_archived` does not exist (archive parity with Member)
**Origin:** drive-by find during `Marina.max_loa` drop (column-drop agent, 2026-05-18).
**Why:** `recalculate_lead_scores` tried to filter `Vessel.objects.filter(is_archived=False)` but the field doesn't exist; the filter falls back to `.all()`. More broadly: `Member` has `is_archived` but `Vessel` doesn't, creating an asymmetry. Archived members appear to still own active vessels.
**Scope:** Add `is_archived = BooleanField(default=False)` + `archived_at` timestamp to `Vessel`. Wire admin/UI archive action. Cascade rule: archiving a Member offers (but does not force) archiving their vessels. Update lead-score and any "active fleet" queries to honor the flag.
**Effort:** ~1 day incl. migration, admin action, and a frontend "Archived" tab on the Vessels screen.

This is an exceptionally clean and well-scoped backlog. Isolating the immediate operational risks from the long-term tech debt ensures the engineering team knows exactly what moves the needle without getting distracted by refactoring tasks.

Here is an architectural review of the backlog, flagging one critical operational risk and one severely underestimated effort calculation.

1. The Critical Path: Elevate F2 (Inventory Bridge)
While F1 is a great quick win for platform leverage, F2 (Phase 3: Inventory Bridge) is a ticking time bomb.

If Phases 1 and 2 of the seasonal tenancy are deployed to production without F2, the Tetris allocation engine is actively blind to seasonal leases. The system will see leased berths as empty transient inventory. This will inevitably result in a double-booked slip and a physical confrontation on the docks between a seasonal leaseholder and a transient guest. F2 must be the absolute highest priority for the next sprint.

2. The Effort Warning: TD2 (Partial Payments)
Your decision to leave TD2 (Partial Payments) in the tech debt pile is the right call, but the effort estimate of "~2 days" is highly optimistic.

Allocating partial payments across multiple line items with differing tax jurisdictions, handling edge cases where a boater disputes a specific line item but pays the rest, and generating compliant partial-receipts is a notorious accounting tar pit. When you do schedule this, budget at least a full week of backend architecture time to ensure the ledger remains balanced.

3. Solid Wins: F1 & F6 (Billing Enforcement)
F1 (Stripe Connect Payout-Block) and F6 (Billing UI) are the perfect pairing to wrap up the billing safety gates.

F1 applies maximum leverage to delinquent accounts without punishing the boaters or cutting off the flow of funds.

F6 prevents angry support tickets by giving the marina owner immediate, transparent context on why their dashboard is restricted.

Together, these represent the gold standard for B2B SaaS dunning enforcement
